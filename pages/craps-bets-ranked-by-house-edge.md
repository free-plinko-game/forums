Craps is a popular casino game characterized by its high-energy atmosphere and a variety of betting options. The game is played with two six-sided dice, and players place bets on the outcome of their rolls. One of the key aspects that players consider while betting is the house edge, which represents the mathematical advantage the casino has over the player for each bet. Understanding the house edge for different craps bets can help players make more informed decisions and potentially improve their odds of success.

### Overview of House Edge

The house edge is a percentage that indicates how much of each bet the casino expects to keep in the long run. Bets with a lower house edge are generally more favorable to players, as they offer a higher probability of winning compared to bets with a higher house edge. In craps, the house edge varies significantly among the different types of bets, and so the selection of bets can heavily influence a player's gaming strategy.

### Bets with the Lowest House Edge

1. **Pass Line Bet / Come Bet**

   - **Description**: The Pass Line bet is the most common bet in craps. It is placed before the come-out roll, and players win if the shooter rolls a 7 or 11 but lose on a 2, 3, or 12. If another number is rolled, that number becomes the "point," and the player wins if the point is rolled again before a 7.
   - **House Edge**: Approximately 1.41%
   
   The Come bet functions similarly but is placed after a point has been established.

2. **Don't Pass Line Bet / Don't Come Bet**

   - **Description**: This is the opposite of the Pass Line bet. Players win if the come-out roll is a 2 or 3 and lose on a 7 or 11. A roll of 12 results in a tie. If the point is established, the player wins if a 7 is rolled before the point number.
   - **House Edge**: Approximately 1.36%

3. **Odds Bet**

   - **Description**: The Odds bet is a supplementary bet that can be placed once a point is established. It is an extension of either the Pass Line or Come bet and has no house edge, as it pays true odds.
   - **House Edge**: 0%
   
   Although the Odds bet has no house edge, players must have a basic bet on the Pass, Don't Pass, Come, or Don't Come lines to take advantage of it.

### Bets with Moderate to High House Edge

1. **Place Bets on 6 or 8**

   - **Description**: Players wager that a 6 or 8 will be rolled before a 7. These are slightly less risky compared to other place bets due to their probability.
   - **House Edge**: Approximately 1.52%

2. **Field Bet**

   - **Description**: This is a single-roll bet where players win if a 2, 3, 4, 9, 10, 11, or 12 is rolled.
   - **House Edge**: Approximately 5.56%

3. **Hardways**

   - **Description**: Hardway bets are wagers that a specific pair of doubles (such as a pair of fours for an 8) will be rolled before a 7 or a non-double equivalent of that number.
   - **House Edge**: Contains a high edge, approximately 9.09% for 6 and 8, and even higher for 4 and 10.

4. **Any Seven**

   - **Description**: This is a one-roll bet which predicts that a 7 will be rolled next.
   - **House Edge**: Approximately 16.67%
