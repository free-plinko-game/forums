## Optimal Play in Video Poker

Video poker is a casino game that combines elements of both slot machines and poker. Unlike traditional poker, video poker is played on a digital console similar to a slot machine. The game typically involves drawing five cards and then choosing which to hold or discard to make the best possible poker hand. Optimal play in video poker is essentially about making mathematically correct decisions to maximize returns over the long term.

### Understanding Pay Tables

A fundamental aspect of optimizing play in video poker is understanding the game's pay table. The pay table provides information on what each winning hand pays relative to the player's bet. Different variations of video poker, such as "Jacks or Better," "Deuces Wild," or "Double Bonus," have unique pay tables, which affect the optimal strategy.

- **Jacks or Better:** In this version, a pair of jacks or better results in a win. The optimal strategy involves understanding the value of each card and how often they appear in the deck.
- **Deuces Wild:** All twos serve as wild cards. This significantly alters strategy because wild cards can substitute for any other card to form a winning hand.
- **Bonus Poker:** This variant often provides higher payouts for certain combinations such as four aces, affecting strategy when such hands become possible.

The payoff for each hand combination directly corresponds to the game's [house edge](./house-edge), impacting the [return-to-player (RTP)](./return-to-player) percentage.

### Strategy Charts

Players often use strategy charts to determine the correct action for any given hand they encounter. These charts break down potential hands and indicate which cards to hold or discard to ensure optimal play. The strategy varies slightly for each variant of video poker due to the differences in pay tables and game rules.

#### Steps to Use a Strategy Chart

1. **Identify the Hand:** Begin by evaluating the initial five cards dealt to you.
2. **Consult the Chart:** Find the closest match on the strategy chart for actions you should take.
3. **Make Your Decision:** Follow the chart's recommendation to either hold or discard cards for the best possible outcome.

### Calculating Expected Value

A critical part of optimal play is understanding the expected value (EV) of a hand. Each decision in video poker has a mathematical expectation of return tied to it, which is calculated using probabilities and payouts from the pay table.

- **Calculating EV:** Determine the probability of drawing specific hands along with the associated payoffs.
- **Maximizing EV:** Choose the action with the highest EV, which theoretically offers the best long-term return.

### Practicing Bankroll Management

Even with a perfect strategy, variance in video poker can lead to fluctuations in a player's bankroll. Effective bankroll management is essential.

- **Set Limits:** Determine a comfortable bankroll and set loss limits to prevent excessive losses.
- **Session Management:** Decide in advance the number of times you will play and how long each session will last.
- **Gradual Stakes:** Start with lower stakes and gradually increase as you become more confident in your ability to play optimally.

### Using Software and Simulators

There are various software tools and online simulators designed to help players practice and refine their video poker strategy. These tools provide hands-on experience with immediate feedback on players' decisions.

- **Simulating Real Play:** Use simulators to practice with the specific variant you plan to play.
- **Correcting Mistakes:** Gain insights into errors by analyzing incorrect decisions.

### Conclusion

Achieving optimal play in video poker is a systematic process that involves understanding pay tables, meticulous use of strategy charts, calculating expected values, and practicing efficient bankroll management. By mastering these techniques, players can significantly enhance their chances of winning and enjoy a more fulfilling gaming experience.
