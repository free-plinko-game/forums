## Red Dog Poker Explained

Red Dog Poker, also known as Yablon or Acey-Deucey, is a casino card game that has captured the interest of many due to its straightforward rules and relatively quick pace. ### Game Setup

Red Dog Poker typically accommodates up to six players, though it can also be enjoyed solo against the house. The main objective is to predict whether the third card dealt will have a value that falls between the first two cards.

#### Deck and Card Values

The game uses a standard 52-card deck without any jokers. Card values follow the traditional poker hierarchy, with aces being high and twos considered low. For example, in a sequence where the cards are a seven and a ten, a card with a value of eight or nine is likely to win. Suits do not play any role in Red Dog Poker.

### Rules of Play

1. **Initial Deal**:
   - Players begin by placing their initial wagers on the table.
   - The dealer then deals two cards face-up and one card face-down.

2. **Spread**:
   - The "spread" refers to the number of card values that exist between the two face-up cards.
   - If the two cards dealt are consecutive (e.g., a four and five), the hand is a push and the player's bet is returned.
   - If the two cards are of the same rank, a third card is dealt immediately. If the third card is the same rank again, the player wins at odds of 11:1. If not, it results in a push.

3. **Player Decision**:
   - Based on the spread, the player decides to either raise their bet or continue without increasing their wager.

4. **Outcome**:
   - The dealer then reveals the face-down card.
   - If the card's value falls between the two face-up cards, the player wins at different odds based on the table's payout structure.
   - If it does not fall within the spread, the player loses their bet.

### Winning Odds

The payouts in Red Dog Poker are determined by the spread:

- **Spread of one**: 5:1 odds
- **Spread of two**: 4:1 odds
- **Spread of three**: 2:1 odds
