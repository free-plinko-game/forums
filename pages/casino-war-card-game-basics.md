## Casino War: Card Game Basics

Casino War is a straightforward card game often found in casinos. It derives from the classic children's game of War, retaining the simple principles that make it accessible and exciting to a broad audience. The game is played using one to six standard decks of 52 cards, where each card maintains its traditional poker ranking, with Aces being high.

### Objective

The primary objective of Casino War is to possess a higher card than the dealer. A simple wager is placed at the outset, and cards are then dealt to both the player and the dealer. If the player's card is higher, they win even money on their bet. Conversely, the player loses if the dealer's card is higher.

### Gameplay

The game starts when a player makes an initial bet on the table. The dealer then deals one card to the player and one to themselves. The comparison is straightforward, with higher cards winning.

#### Ties

In the event of ties, where the player's card and the dealer's card are of equal value, the player has two options:
1. **Go to War**: The player must double their initial bet. The dealer will then "burn" three cards and place a fourth card face up for both the player and the dealer. If the player's new card is higher or equal to the dealer's, the player wins the war and receives even money on the raised bet. If the dealer's card is higher, the player loses both bets.
2. **Surrender**: The player forfeits half of their initial wager and ends the round.

### Side Bets

Many variations of Casino War offer a side bet known as the *tie bet*. This bet predicts an initial tie between the player and dealer's cards. Players who place a successful tie bet typically receive payouts ranging anywhere from 10:1 to 20:1, increasing the excitement and the potential for higher returns.

### Strategy

Casino War is largely a game of chance with minimal strategy involved, primarily due to the absence of decision-making beyond the initial bet and tie scenarios. However, players typically choose the "war" option over surrendering as it improves the chances of breaking even or potentially winning the round instead of conceding part of their bet outright.

### House Edge

The simplicity of Casino War results in a relatively low house edge compared to many other casino games. When playing optimally (choosing 'war' in the case of ties), the house edge is typically around 2% to 2.5%. The inclusion of a tie bet can significantly increase this edge to approximately 18%, depending on payout conditions.

### Variations

While the traditional gameplay of Casino War remains consistent, some casinos offer variations with adjusted payout structures or bonuses for certain card matchups or high card rankings. Players should familiarize themselves with these local rule variations prior to playing.
