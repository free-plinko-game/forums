## Craps: A Complete Introduction

Craps is a popular casino dice game known for its fast pace and vibrant atmosphere. Played on a specially-designed table with two six-sided dice, craps is distinguished by a variety of betting options and complex procedures. Its origins can be traced back to the English game of hazard, which was popularized in the United States in the early 1800s. Over time, it evolved into the modern game of craps, which is now a staple in casinos around the world.

### Basic Objective

The primary objective in craps is to predict the outcome of each dice roll. Players take turns rolling the dice, and the player who rolls is known as the "shooter." The game revolves around rounds of play, with the initial roll being termed the "come-out roll." Players can place wagers on the results of the come-out roll or subsequent rolls in the round.

### Game Structure and Bets

The game of craps is structured around two main phases: the "come-out roll" and the "point." The shooter initiates the round with the come-out roll. If the come-out roll results in a 7 or 11, bets on the "Pass Line" win. However, if the outcome is a 2, 3, or 12, Pass Line bets lose, and the round concludes. Any other number rolled sets the "point," and the game proceeds to the next phase.

During the point phase, the shooter continues to roll the dice until either the point number is rolled again, resulting in a win for Pass Line bets, or a 7 is rolled, which ends the round with a loss for these bets. 

#### Types of Bets

1. **Pass Line Bet**: The most fundamental wager in craps, it wins if the come-out roll is 7 or 11 and loses on a 2, 3, or 12.
2. **Don't Pass Line Bet**: Essentially the opposite of the Pass Line bet, winning on a 2 or 3 and losing on a 7 or 11. A roll of 12 results in a push (neither winning nor losing).
3. **Come Bet**: Similar to the Pass Line bet but made after the point is established. Wins on the next roll if it is 7 or 11 and loses with a 2, 3, or 12.
4. **Don't Come Bet**: Opposite of the Come bet, winning on a 2 or 3 and losing on a 7 or 11. A 12 results in a push.
5. **Odds Bets**: An additional wager placed behind a Pass, Don't Pass, Come, or Don't Come bet after the point is established, with payout odds reflecting the true likelihood of winning.
6. **Place Bets**: Wagers made directly on certain numbers (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10). If the bet number is rolled before a 7, the bet wins; otherwise, it loses.

### Strategies and Etiquette

Success in craps often involves a combination of understanding the game's odds and adhering to strategic betting systems. Players are advised to familiarize themselves with the house edge associated with different bets; for instance, Pass Line and Don't Pass Line bets tend to have a lower house edge compared to more exotic wagers.

Craps tables are often lively environments with unwritten rules of etiquette. Common practices include handling the dice with one hand only, throwing the dice hard enough to reach the far end of the table, and avoiding superstitions that some players might take seriously, such as mentioning the number seven explicitly.
