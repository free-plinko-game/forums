## Roulette Wheel Layouts Compared

Roulette is a popular casino game with a rich history and variations that appeal to different player preferences. The layout of the roulette wheel is one of the main aspects distinguishing each variant. This article compares the layouts of the three most common types of roulette wheels: European, American, and French. Understanding these differences can aid players in choosing their preferred version and inform strategies applied at the table.

### European Roulette

The European roulette wheel features 37 numbered pockets, which include numbers 1 through 36 and a single zero (`0`). The numbers are alternately colored red and black, except for the single zero, which is green. This configuration provides a house edge of 2.7%, which is slightly more advantageous for players compared to American roulette.

#### Number Arrangement

The numbers on the European roulette wheel are arranged in a specific sequence designed to balance odd and even numbers, as well as the red and black distribution. This arrangement without any consecutive numbers and evenly spread colors is engineered to maintain randomness.

#### Betting Options

Players in European roulette have a diverse range of betting possibilities, including inside and outside bets. Popular outside bets include red or black, odd or even, and high or low numbers, each covering various sections of the wheel.

### American Roulette

The American roulette wheel comprises 38 pockets, featuring numbers 1 through 36, a single zero (`0`), and a double zero (`00`). This addition of a double zero increases the house edge to 5.26%, which is less favorable to players compared to the European variant.

#### Number Arrangement

Unlike the European wheel, the American wheel attempts to achieve balance in the placement of consecutive and alternating numbers but introduces additional complexity with the double zero. Each segment of the wheel thereby affects potential betting outcomes.

#### Additional Bets

In addition to classic bets seen in European roulette, American roulette offers unique inside bets such as the "five number" bet. This bet covers the numbers 0, 00, 1, 2, and 3, but is known for having a particularly high house edge.

### French Roulette

French roulette is similar to the European version in having 37 numbers, including a single zero. However, it introduces distinct rules such as "La Partage" and "En Prison" that can significantly affect player strategy and outcome.

#### Number Arrangement

The number arrangement on the French roulette wheel mirrors that of the European variant. However, unique table layouts distinguish it from its counterparts, often featuring French terminology for the betting areas.

#### Special Rules

- **La Partage**: This rule allows players to recover half their even-money bets if the ball lands on zero, effectively reducing the house edge to 1.35% on these bets.
- **En Prison**: Similar to "La Partage," if a player makes an even-money bet and the ball lands on zero, the bet is not lost but is held "in prison" for the next spin. If it wins on the following spin, the bet is returned to the player without a win.

### Comparison Summary

- **House Edge**: European roulette offers a lower house edge at 2.7% compared to American roulette's 5.26%. French roulette can provide an exceptionally low house edge on even-money bets due to special rules.
- **Zero Pockets**: American roulette features both single and double zeros, while the European and French versions feature a single zero.
- **Betting Variety**: While all variants offer a wide array of betting options, the rules applicable in French roulette add strategic depth.

Each roulette type provides a unique experience influenced by regional preferences and specific rules. Players often select the version that best aligns with their strategies and desired odds.
