## Three Card Poker: How to Play

Three Card Poker is a popular casino table game that combines the strategy of poker with the simplicity and speed of a casino card game. Invented by Derek Webb in 1994, it has become a staple in many casinos worldwide due to its easy-to-understand rules and swift gameplay. This guide provides an overview of the game's rules, the betting options available, and basic strategy tips for players.

### Game Objective

The main objective in Three Card Poker is to make the best possible poker hand with three cards, both in the player's hand and the dealer's. Unlike traditional poker, the player competes directly against the dealer rather than against other players at the table.

### Basic Rules

Three Card Poker is played with a standard deck of 52 cards. Before the cards are dealt, players must place an initial wager known as the **Ante** bet. An optional **Pair Plus** side bet can also be placed before the deal, wagering on whether the player's hand will contain a pair or better.

1. **Dealing the Cards**: After the bets are placed, both the player and dealer receive three cards each. All cards are dealt face down.
   
2. **Analyzing the Hand**: Players must decide whether to fold or continue playing after reviewing their cards.
   - **Fold**: The player forfeits the Ante bet if they choose to fold.
   - **Play Bet**: If continuing, the player must place a second bet equal to the Ante amount to see the dealer's hand.

3. **Dealer's Qualification**: For the dealer’s hand to qualify, it must contain at least a Queen high. If the dealer does not qualify, the player wins even money on the Ante bet, and the Play bet is returned.

4. **Comparing Hands**: If the dealer qualifies, the player’s hand is compared to the dealer’s hand:
   - **Player Wins**: Both the Ante and Play bets pay even money.
   - **Dealer Wins**: The player loses both bets.
   - **Tie**: If there is a tie, the Ante and Play bets are returned to the player.

5. **Ante Bonus**: Players may receive an Ante bonus for certain high-ranking hands, such as a straight, three of a kind, or a straight flush. This bonus is paid regardless of whether the dealer qualifies or whether the player wins or loses the hand.

### Poker Hand Rankings

The hand rankings used in Three Card Poker differ slightly from those in traditional poker due to the three-card hand size. The standard rankings, from highest to lowest, are as follows:

1. **Straight Flush**: Three consecutive cards of the same suit.
2. **Three of a Kind**: Three cards of the same rank.
3. **Straight**: Three consecutive cards of different suits.
4. **Flush**: Three cards of the same suit.
5. **Pair**: Two cards of the same rank.
6. **High Card**: The highest card when no other hand is made.

While Three Card Poker is fundamentally a game of chance, incorporating a few strategic decisions can enhance a player’s experience:

- Consider folding hands that are weaker than a Queen-Six-Four. This threshold is based on statistical probabilities and can help minimize losses over time.
- Utilize the Pair Plus side bet cautiously, as while it can offer large payouts, it also has a higher house edge compared to the main game.
- Take advantage of the Ante Bonus by playing hands that have the potential to earn this extra payout, such as straights or better.
