### Origins and Early Development

The origins of video poker can be traced back to the 1970s, a period of burgeoning interest in electronic gaming. The first semblance of video poker emerged in 1970, when Dale Electronics introduced the "Poker-Matic" machine. This initial machine, however, lacked the sophistication of today's games; it consisted of a television monitor and a central processing unit, and players interacted with it via simple electro-mechanical controls.

The true breakthrough for video poker occurred in 1979, when the gaming company SIRCOMA, later known as International Game Technology (IGT), launched "Draw Poker." Combining a television screen with a microprocessor, Draw Poker offered five card draw poker and featured a novel player-friendly interface. This innovation significantly contributed to the acceptance and popularity of video poker in casinos.

### Technological Advancements in the 1980s and 1990s

The 1980s marked an era of rapid growth for video poker, largely driven by advancements in technology and increased accessibility in casinos. During this decade, video poker machines became more sophisticated, incorporating features like color graphics and improved user interfaces. These advancements made the game more appealing and easier for gamblers to play, enhancing their overall gambling experience.

A pivotal addition to the video poker machine repertoire during the late 1980s and 1990s was the introduction of different poker variations. Games such as "Jacks or Better," "Deuces Wild," and "Joker Poker" became widespread. These variations not only provided players with more choices but also increased their engagement by offering different strategies and payout structures.
