### Basic Rules

Baccarat is played with a standard deck of 52 cards, though casinos often use multiple decks shuffled together. The primary objective is to predict which hand, the Player's or the Banker's, will have a total closest to nine. Additionally, players can also bet on a tie if they believe both hands will have the same value.

#### Card Values

- Number cards (2-9) are worth their face value.
- Aces are worth one point.
- Tens and face cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks) are worth zero points.

The hand value is determined by summing the card values and taking only the last digit. For example, a hand comprised of a 7 and 8 has a total of 15, which counts as 5 in Baccarat.

#### Gameplay

1. **Place Bets:** Before the cards are dealt, players place their bets on the Player, Banker, or Tie.
2. **Deal Cards:** The dealer distributes two cards each to the Player and Banker positions.

4. **Third Card Rule:** If there is no natural win, the dealer may draw a third card based on specific rules:

Several variations of Baccarat are played in casinos around the world, including:

- **Baccarat Banque:** Similar to Chemin de Fer, but the Banker position is more permanent throughout the shoe.
