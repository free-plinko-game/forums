## Pontoon: The British Cousin of Blackjack

Pontoon is a popular card game primarily played in the United Kingdom and other parts of the British Commonwealth. It shares a close resemblance to Blackjack, a game with more global recognition, yet retains distinct rules and features that set it apart. This game of chance and strategy has been a staple in British gaming circles and continues to enjoy popularity in online casinos and home game settings.

### Objective of the Game

The primary objective in Pontoon is identical to Blackjack: players aim to form a hand with a total value as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it. However, Pontoon introduces some unique terminologies and strategic nuances that distinguish it from its American counterpart.

### Basic Gameplay

#### The Deck and Cards

Pontoon uses one or more standard 52-card decks. The cards in Pontoon carry values similar to those in Blackjack:
- Numbered cards carry their face value.
- Face cards (King, Queen, and Jack) are worth 10 points.
- Aces can be worth either 1 or 11 points, depending on what benefits the player's hand most.

#### Key Terms

Pontoon employs different terminology from Blackjack, adopting names such as:
- **Pontoon**: An Ace and any ten-point card in a player's first two cards, equivalent to a "Blackjack."
- **Five Card Trick**: A hand consisting of five cards with a total value of 21 or under. It is considered the second-highest ranking hand, below a Pontoon.
- **Twist**: The action of taking an additional card, similar to "Hit" in Blackjack.
- **Stick**: Holds the same meaning as "Stand," where the player decides to keep their current hand and not take more cards.
- **Buy**: An option allowing players to double their initial bet. After "buying," they receive one additional card.

### The Rules of the Game

In Pontoon, all players, including the banker, are dealt two cards at the beginning of the game. Both dealer cards are kept face down, contrary to Blackjack where one card is exposed. This increases uncertainty and alters the strategic elements of the game.

#### Winning Conditions

A player achieves victory by:
1. Forming a Pontoon with the initial two cards.
2. Creating a Five Card Trick.
3. Having a higher hand than the dealer without exceeding 21.

#### Special Rules

- **Doubling Down**: Known as "buying" in Pontoon. Players can also buy cards during their turn, not restricted to their first two cards as in Blackjack.
- **Dealer Constraints**: The dealer must twist until reaching at least 16. Interestingly, the dealer in Pontoon must also stick on a "soft 17", unlike in [Blackjack](./blackjack), where they often hit on soft 17.
- **Push Condition**: In case of a tie, the dealer wins, a rule favoring the house more than in Blackjack.

### Strategy Tips

Adopting effective strategy in Pontoon requires an understanding of its variance from Blackjack:
- Aim for a Five Card Trick when possible, as it offers a higher probability of winning, especially when the dealer's cards remain hidden.
- Be judicious with buying cards; adding cards late in the game can improve a weak hand.
- Practice managing funds as the "buy" action can require high costs.

### Conclusion

In conclusion, Pontoon serves as a thrilling variant of Blackjack, offering familiar yet distinct features that appeal to both novice players and seasoned card enthusiasts. Its unique ruleset and the strategic depth provided by the hidden dealer's hand keep players engaged and slightly on edge. Whether participating in a casual game among friends or playing in an online setting, understanding Pontoon's nuances can greatly enhance a player's experience.
