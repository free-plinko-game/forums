## Faro: A Forgotten Casino Classic

Faro, often described as a staple of 19th-century gambling establishments, is a card game that once rivaled poker and [blackjack](https://www.sunvegascasino.com/casino-games/blackjack/) in popularity. Known for its simple rules and fast-paced action, Faro's influence can still be felt in modern casino games, though its presence has largely faded away from contemporary gambling venues.

### Historical Background

Faro, also known as "Pharaoh" in some regions, dates back to the 17th century, with its origins likely tied to France. The game gained its name from the image of a Pharaoh on one of the oldest forms of French playing cards. As it migrated to the United States, Faro quickly became a favorite among gambling enthusiasts during the American Gold Rush and throughout the Western frontier. By the late 19th century, it was a dominant fixture in saloons and gambling halls, often associated with the image of the daring gamblers of the Old West.

### Gameplay Mechanics

Faro is a relatively simple game when compared to other casino classics. It is played with a standard 52-card deck without any jokers. The game accommodates multiple players, and the primary equipment in use includes a layout or board featuring 13 cards, representing each rank from ace through king.

1. **Objective**: Players place bets on which card ranks will win in a duel between two drawn cards.
2. **Banker and Players**: One participant takes the role of the 'banker' while the others are players who place bets on the layout.
3. **Drawing Process**: The banker draws two cards from the dealing box on each turn—a 'winning' and a 'losing' card.
4. **Winning and Losing Bets**: Bets placed on the rank of the winning card pay even money, while bets on the losing card result in a loss.
5. **Variation in Rules**: Regional variations existed, including differences in payout structures and the handling of ‘split’ bets, where players would bet on two adjacent cards.

### Factors Leading to Decline

Several factors contributed to the decline of Faro into obscurity:

- **Cheating and Fraud**: Due to its straightforward nature, Faro was notorious for being susceptible to cheating. Unscrupulous dealers and players often manipulated the game, leading to its reputation as a crooked pastime.
- **Rise of Poker and Blackjack**: As poker and blackjack gained massive popularity in the 20th century, Faro's appeal waned significantly. These games offered more complex strategies and betting options, drawing players away from Faro.
- **Regulatory Changes**: Stringent gambling laws and the rise of regulated casinos also played a role in the decline of Faro, as it couldn't compete with the attractions promoted by new and seemingly safer games.

### Legacy and Modern Relevance

Despite its decline, Faro remains an influential part of gambling history. Its mechanics influenced the development of other gambling games, and its romanticized image of the Old West persists in popular culture. References to Faro can still be found in historical films and literature, highlighting its role in the gambling scene of yesteryears.

Though Faro is virtually absent from contemporary casinos, it enjoys a modest revival among card game enthusiasts and historical societies. Periodic Faro games take place in historical reenactments and special events to offer a glimpse of the once-popular game to new audiences.

### Conclusion

Faro's journey from a widely played game to relative obscurity highlights the dynamics of the gambling industry. Its simplicity, coupled with an open susceptibility to cheating, both propelled and doomed its widespread popularity. As a cultural artifact, Faro serves as a reminder of the ever-evolving landscape of casino games and the fleeting nature of what was once considered cutting-edge entertainment.
