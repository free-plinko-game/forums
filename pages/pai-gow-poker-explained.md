## Pai Gow Poker Explained

Pai Gow Poker is a casino card game that combines elements of the ancient Chinese game of Pai Gow and traditional poker. It is a game of skill and strategy, often enjoyed by players who appreciate a slower pace and a lower volatility in their gambling experiences. The game is typically found in casinos throughout the world and offers players a unique alternative to more fast-paced card games, such as blackjack and [Texas Hold'em](https://www.sunvegascasino.com/online-poker/texas-holdem/).

### History and Origin

Pai Gow Poker originated in the United States in the 1980s. It was created by Sam Torosian, the owner of the Bell Card Club in California, who sought to introduce a game that combined the appeal of poker with the strategic complexity of Pai Gow. Pai Gow itself is a domino game that dates back to the Song Dynasty in China. By fusing these two game types, Torosian constructed a new casino card game that gained popularity quickly in North America and eventually spread to casinos worldwide.

### Gameplay

Pai Gow Poker is played using a standard 52-card deck plus one joker. The game can accommodate up to six players and one dealer, each striving to beat the dealer's hand.

#### The Objective

The primary goal of Pai Gow Poker is to create two hands out of seven cards: a five-card "high" hand and a two-card "low" hand. The player's high hand must rank higher than their low hand. To win, both of the player's hands must beat the dealer’s corresponding hands.

#### Card Rankings

In Pai Gow Poker, the standard poker hand rankings apply, with a few exceptions:

- The joker serves as a wild card that can complete a straight, flush, or serve as an ace.
- The highest possible hand is a five aces combination, which is made possible by using the joker.

#### Dealing and Play

1. **Dealing**: Each player, including the dealer, is dealt seven cards.
2. **Arranging Hands**: Players arrange their cards into a five-card high hand and a two-card low hand.
3. **Comparison**: Once all players have set their hands, they compare them to the dealer's hands.
   - If both player hands beat both dealer hands, the player wins.
   - If both player hands lose to the dealer's hands, the player loses.
   - If one hand wins and the other loses, the hand is considered a push, and the player's bet is returned.
   - Should any rules be tied (exact matching hands), the dealer’s hand wins.

### Strategy

Strategic play in Pai Gow Poker involves understanding when to break up strong hands to ensure both the high hand and the low hand are competitive. Specific strategies include:

- **Splitting Pairs**: It may be advantageous to break pairs to strengthen the low hand, especially when dealt two pair.
- **Conserving Poker Hands**: High-ranking poker hands, such as straights and flushes, should often remain in the high hand unless breaking them benefits the overall hand structure.
- **Optimal Low Hand**: Given the strength of typical low hands, a common strategy is to place the second and third best cards in the low hand when possible.
