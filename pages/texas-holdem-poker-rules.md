## Texas Hold'em Poker Rules

Texas Hold'em is one of the most popular variations of poker, widely played in casinos and online platforms worldwide. The game is recognized for its strategic depth and combination of luck, skill, and psychology.

### Objective

The primary objective in Texas Hold'em is to win chips by either having the highest-ranking hand or by forcing all opponents to fold their cards. Each player seeks to form the best possible five-card poker hand using any combination of the two private cards dealt to them and the five community cards.

### Game Setup

1. **Deck and Players**: Texas Hold'em uses a standard 52-card deck and can accommodate 2 to 10 players.
2. **Dealer Button**: A dealer button rotates clockwise after each hand, indicating the nominal dealer position, which determines the order of betting and dealing.
3. **Blinds**: Two mandatory bets are placed before dealing, known as the small blind and the big blind. The player to the left of the dealer posts the small blind and the next player posts the big blind.

### Dealing Cards

The dealer distributes cards in the following sequence:

1. **Hole Cards**: Each player receives two private cards, face-down, known as hole cards.
2. **Community Cards**: Five community cards are dealt face-up in the center of the table in three stages: the "flop" (three cards), the "turn" (one card), and the "river" (one card).

### Betting Rounds

Texas Hold'em features four betting rounds:

1. **Pre-Flop**: Players bet based on the strength of their hole cards. Betting starts with the player to the left of the big blind.
2. **Flop**: After the first betting round, three community cards are dealt. A second round of betting ensues, starting with the player to the left of the dealer.
3. **Turn**: A fourth community card is added to the table, followed by a third round of betting.
4. **River**: The fifth and final community card is revealed, followed by the final betting round.

### Betting Options

During each betting round, players can choose from several actions:

- **Fold**: Discard their hand and forfeit any chance of winning the current pot.
- **Check**: Pass the action to the next player without betting; available only if no previous bet has been made in the current round.
- **Bet/Raise**: Place a new bet or increase the current bet amount.
- **Call**: Match the current bet to stay in the hand.

### Winning the Hand

A showdown occurs if more than one player remains after the final betting round. Players reveal their hole cards, and the best five-card hand wins the pot. The hand rankings, from highest to lowest, are as follows:

1. **Royal Flush**: Ten, Jack, Queen, King, Ace of the same suit.
2. **Straight Flush**: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
3. **Four of a Kind**: Four cards of the same rank.
4. **Full House**: Three cards of one rank and two cards of another rank.
5. **Flush**: Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.
6. **Straight**: Five consecutive cards of different suits.
7. **Three of a Kind**: Three cards of the same rank.
8. **Two Pair**: Two different pairs of cards.
9. **One Pair**: Two cards of the same rank.
10. **High Card**: The highest single card available if no other hand is made.

### Strategy and Variation

Texas Hold'em is renowned for its strategic complexity. Players must weigh numerous factors, including position, opponent behavior, and betting patterns, to make informed decisions. While luck can be a factor in the short term, skillful play can have a more significant impact over time.

### Texas Hold'em Variations

While this guide outlines the basic rules of No-Limit Texas Hold'em, there are other forms, such as Limit and Pot-Limit Hold'em, which modify betting structures and strategies accordingly.
