## Caribbean Stud Poker Rules

Caribbean Stud Poker is a popular casino table game that combines elements from the traditional poker. Unlike most poker games, Caribbean Stud Poker is played against the house rather than against other players. This guide outlines the basic rules of the game, the terms commonly used, and the structure of gameplay.

### Game Objective

The primary objective of Caribbean Stud Poker is to achieve a higher-ranking poker hand than the dealer's hand. The game utilizes a standard 52-card deck with no jokers. 

### Basic Rules

1. **Ante and Bet Placement**: Each player begins by placing an ante wager. Optionally, a player can also place a progressive side bet, typically leading to higher payouts for certain hands. 

2. **Dealing the Cards**: The dealer provides five cards face down to each player and himself. Players can examine their own cards, while the dealer’s last card is dealt face up for all to see. 

3. **Player Options**: Players must decide whether to fold or continue playing. To continue, players place an additional wager, called a "raise", typically equal to twice the ante. 
   - **Fold**: If the player folds, they forfeit their ante.
   - **Raise**: If the player chooses to raise, they place the appropriate wager on the table.

4. **Dealer Qualification**: To qualify, the dealer's hand must contain an Ace-King or higher combination. If the dealer does not qualify, players win even money on their ante, and the raise bet is returned.

5. **Comparing Hands**: If the dealer qualifies, the dealer's and player's hands are compared:
   - **Player Winning**: The player receives even money on the ante and a variable payout on the raise, based on the strength of their hand. Payouts for the raise wager typically follow a set paytable, with higher amounts awarded for rare hands.
   - **Dealer Winning**: The player loses both the ante and raise bets.
   - **Tie**: If there is a tie, both the ante and the raise are returned to the player.

### Hand Rankings

The rank of hands in Caribbean Stud Poker is based on traditional poker hand rankings, as follows from highest to lowest:
   
- **Royal Flush**: A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
- **Straight Flush**: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
- **Four of a Kind**: Four cards of the same rank.
- **Full House**: Three of a kind and a pair.
- **Flush**: Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.
- **Straight**: Five consecutive cards of different suits.
- **Three of a Kind**: Three cards of the same rank.
- **Two Pair**: Two different pairs.
- **One Pair**: Two cards of the same rank.
- **High Card**: The hand with the highest card when no other hand is achieved.

### Progressive Jackpot

Many casinos offer a progressive jackpot as part of Caribbean Stud Poker, offering players a chance to win large payouts with an additional side bet. Typically, such bets pay out according to a secondary paytable, rewarding hands such as flushes and above.

### Strategies and Tips

Several basic strategies can help improve a player's odds in Caribbean Stud Poker:

- **Play with an Ace-King**: Continue playing when holding a hand with at least Ace-King, even with lower cards.
- **Avoid the Progressive Bet**: While attractive, the odds are generally against winning the progressive jackpot.
- **Fold Weak Hands Early**: It is generally advisable to fold without at least Ace-King or higher.

### Conclusion

Caribbean Stud Poker provides an engaging variant of traditional poker, pitting players against the dealer rather than each other. The rules are designed to make gambling straightforward yet entertaining, offering opportunities for strategic decision-making and interaction with the common poker hand rankings.
